The Second Budget

Rethink the gifting budget.

Most corporate gifting still runs on leather, silk and wool. It is often a larger line than CSR, and nothing in Schedule VII touches it. That makes it the easiest place to bring your gifting into line with your values, on every desk, every year.

What we replace it with

  • Nothing animal-based. No leather, no silk, no wool. A guardrail, not a preference.
  • Every unit funds rescue. The margin is the point, not a rounding error.
  • An impact receipt in every box, so the recipient sees the animal their gift helped.
  • Co-branded to whatever degree you want, or not at all.

Why it is worth more than the logo

  • 50,000 desks is 50,000 people reading your position, not just your name.
  • The gift is the campaign. Nobody skips it, and nobody scrolls past it.
  • It is the rare piece of corporate gifting a recipient keeps and photographs.
  • Two budgets, one relationship.

The Return

Certify your own campuses.

PFA X is our public mark for places that treat animals with dignity. Your offices, townships, canteens and plants can be assessed against it. It cannot be bought at any price, by anyone, which is precisely why it is worth carrying.

How it is earned

  • Certified: documents verified against the issuing authority, approved by PFA.
  • Gold: twelve months held, attested by verified people who live nearby, zero upheld reports.
  • Platinum: thirty-six months of the same, unbroken.
  • One credible report from a verified neighbour moves the mark to Under review that day.

Why that matters to you

  • No fee shortens it. Nobody at PFA can waive it. There is no tier to purchase.
  • The register is public and dated, and shows revocations beside awards.
  • A mark that can be taken away is the only kind worth putting on a wall.
  • Your money and your own estate are held to one standard.

The Boring Part, Which Decides Everything

Animal welfare is in the statute.

"Ensuring environmental sustainability, ecological balance, protection of flora and fauna, animal welfare, agroforestry, conservation of natural resources and maintaining quality of soil, air and water."

Schedule VII, item (iv) · Companies Act 2013

Animal welfare is often assumed to sit outside CSR. It does not. Your obligation is already set by Section 135 at two per cent; underspending carries a penalty of twice the shortfall, up to a crore; and unspent funds move to a government account unless the project is ongoing. We are not asking you to find new money. We are offering a place to direct the money you are committed to spend anyway.
