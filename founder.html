The Founding

One person,
then a country.

In 1992 there was no network. An injured animal's fate depended entirely on which street it fell on. She decided that was not acceptable - and set about building the country an answer.

What began as one organisation grew into hospitals, shelters, ambulances and units across India - and into something harder to build than any of them: the idea that an animal's suffering is everyone's business.

Thirty-four years on, the hand is still open.

The Core

Many hands.
One line.

Names, because the work has them. These are the hands that have been on it for decades - in court, on the road, and at the end of the phone at two in the morning. One line, and somebody picks it up.

GM

Gauri Maulekhi

Trustee

With PFA since 1995, and founder of its Uttarakhand unit. Took the case against mass religious slaughter at the Gadhimai festival across the border into Nepal, and led the evacuation of more than 6,000 animals from the Kedarnath region during the 2013 floods, which changed how national disaster plans treat animals. Nari Shakti Puraskar.
