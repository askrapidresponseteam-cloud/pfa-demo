The Citation

Dr. Subramaniam Iyer

Community Medicine and Animal Birth Control · Chennai

For thirty-one years, Dr. Iyer has treated the animals no one sends a bill for. He performed his first street-dog sterilisation in 1995 on a kitchen table in Mylapore; he has since led more than sixty thousand, trained four hundred surgeons in the humane catching and handling standard that now carries his name, and never once charged a community animal's caretaker. When the courts asked what a lawful, kind ABC programme looks like, they were shown his operating theatre. The Hall exists for careers like this one.

The Canon

How a name enters the Hall.

Article I

Nominated by the field

Only rescuers, shelters and fellow doctors may nominate. No self-nominations, no institutional lobbying, no campaigns.

Article II

A decade of service, minimum

Ten years of verifiable practice before a name is even read aloud. The Hall honours careers, not moments.

Article III

A pro bono record, verified

PFA independently verifies the unbilled work - the street animals, the wildlife, the shelters. This is the heart of the honour.

Article IV

Elected unanimously

The council of past laureates and PFA trustees must be unanimous. One reservation, and the name waits another year.

No fees. No sponsorships. No exceptions.
