Score all five action units to get a reading.
These five action units are not a PFA invention. They come from the Feline Grimace Scale, developed and validated at the Université de Montréal in 2019, and tested against expert veterinarians. Each unit scores 0 to 2, for a maximum of 10, and a total of 4 or more is the point at which a cat should receive pain relief. It is validated for sudden, acute pain, not the slow pain of arthritis. The official scoring images are free at felinegrimacescale.com. This trainer teaches the method. It does not replace a vet.

The law library · by animal

The law also reads them one at a time.

India writes its animal law species by species - one set of rules for the dog outside your gate, another for the bird in a cage. This shelf holds what actually stands today, statute by statute, judgment by judgment, in plain language. Checked against the statute book and the live Supreme Court record.

The dog file

The most litigated animal in India. One constitutional duty, two criminal codes, a dedicated set of rules and a live Supreme Court bench decide what may and may not be done to a dog - the one on the street and the one on your sofa.

CheckedJuly 2026

Before any rule about streets or leashes, three things hold everywhere in India, for every dog.

Art 51A(g)

The Constitution makes compassion for living creatures a fundamental duty of every citizen. Not a suggestion - a duty, in the same article as respecting the flag.

PCA Act 1960

Section 3 puts a duty of care on anyone in charge of an animal. Section 11 lists the offences: beating, kicking, torturing, starving, overloading, chaining without food, water or shelter, abandoning, poisoning, mutilating.

SC, 2014

In AWBI v. A. Nagaraja the Supreme Court read the five freedoms into Indian law - freedom from hunger and thirst, from discomfort, from pain, injury and disease, from fear and distress, and the freedom to express natural behaviour.

SC, May 2026

The current frame: animal welfare is of real constitutional weight, but it cannot eclipse the state's duty to protect human life under Article 21. Every rule below now operates inside that balance.

Since July 2024 the new criminal code treats violence against a dog as a crime in its own right - and it no longer matters what the animal was worth.

BNS s.325

Killing, poisoning, maiming or rendering any animal useless is punishable with imprisonment of up to five years. The old value thresholds of the IPC are gone - the section protects the animal, not its price tag.

BNS s.291

The section that reaches a negligent owner - keeping an animal in a way that endangers human life or risks grievous hurt is itself an offence.

PCA s.11

Runs alongside for the full cruelty catalogue - including abandonment, which people rarely realise is listed as an offence by name.

BNSS 2023

How to act on it: report at any police station - killing or maiming an animal is a case the police can register an FIR for and investigate. If they refuse, the code gives you the written escalation route to the Superintendent, and then to the Magistrate.

Between July 2025 and May 2026 the Supreme Court rebuilt this ground in In Re: City Hounded by Strays, Kids Pay Price. This is where it landed.

ABC Rules 2023

The only lawful management of street dogs is capture, sterilise, vaccinate, return, run by the local body through AWBI-recognised organisations, with trained catchers, humane catching methods and catch records. Culling is not an option the law gives any municipality, and dumping dogs in another locality is illegal - Rule 11(19) requires release in the same place they were picked up from.

Machinery first

The framework runs in one direction only: a working sterilisation programme and compliant shelters come before any pickup. The AWBI's procedure sets the shelter standards - defined capacities, fencing, kennels, veterinary access - and requires vaccination and sterilisation before sheltering. A removal drive without that machinery behind it is not enforcement of the law. It is a violation of it.

SC, Aug 2025

An order to remove Delhi's dogs to shelters lasted eleven days. The three-judge bench restored sterilise-and-return, and took the matter pan-India.

SC, May 2026

One carve-out, now settled: sterilised dogs are not re-released inside institutional premises - schools and colleges, hospitals, sports complexes, airports, bus stands and railway stations - and the AWBI's operating procedure validly extends this to religious sites, parks and tourist locations. Even there, removal runs only through the same machinery - the local body's teams, sterilisation and vaccination first, then a shelter built to the prescribed standards. Everywhere else, return to territory stands, and the High Courts now monitor compliance district by district.

No private hands

A dog resting somewhere - a corner, a market, a platform - is not, by that fact, anyone's to seize. The capture power belongs to the local authority's trained teams alone, operating under the Rules. A guard, a contractor, a society, a shopkeeper or an angry neighbour who catches, cages or carts a dog away is acting wholly outside the law, whatever the location.

Rule 7(2)

The Court held the street dog category is classificatory, not a right to occupy any particular space. Managing the population humanely and scientifically is the state's continuing duty - and officials who ignore it were expressly warned of contempt, disciplinary action and personal liability.

Euthanasia

Permitted only for rabid, incurably ill or demonstrably dangerous dogs, only after assessment by qualified veterinarians, strictly under the Act and the Rules. Nothing in the judgment licenses killing a healthy dog.

Feeding community dogs is part of the statutory scheme, not an act of defiance. The law asks one thing in return: structure.

ABC r.20

Local bodies and resident associations must designate feeding spots for community animals. Feeding at a designated spot is lawful by rule, not by anyone's permission.

SC + AWBI SOP

The Court has restricted free-for-all street feeding: use the designated spots, keep them clean. The AWBI's November 2025 procedure sets the feeding protocols and waste handling that municipalities must run.

Campuses

Inside educational institutions, feeding or maintaining dogs now requires the group or students to file a liability affidavit with the head of the institution - no affidavit, no feeding programme. That is the price the Court set for the right to care on campus.

BNS s.351

A feeder at a designated spot is doing something the rules provide for. Threats and intimidation against a lawful feeder are criminal intimidation under section 351 - document everything, and report it as the offence it is.

Societies have taken to writing their own animal law - circulars banning feeding, guards told to chase dogs out, catchers hired off the market. Here is the entire extent of what the law actually gives an RWA.

ABC r.20

The RWA's role, in full: designate feeding spots for the community animals of its area and cooperate with the local body's sterilisation programme. That is the list. There is no clause anywhere that hands a society a capture power, a relocation power, or a power to legislate dogs out of existence.

Bye-laws

A society's bye-laws and circulars sit under the statute, not above it. A resolution cannot ban pets, bar them from lifts, evict them, ban lawful feeding, or fine feeders - the AWBI's 2015 advisory says so in terms, and no general-body vote changes what a rule of law requires.

R.11(19) + PCA s.11

Hiring a private catcher, or having guards remove and dump dogs elsewhere, is illegal relocation plus cruelty - and if a dog is hurt in the process, section 325 of the BNS is squarely attracted, for the committee members who ordered it as much as the hands that did it.

BNS s.223

Where the municipality or district administration has issued orders under this framework - designated feeding spots, ABC directions, removal protocols - a society that defies them and runs its own regime is disobeying an order duly promulgated by a public servant, an offence in itself under section 223.

In one line

An RWA may feed, at designated spots, and cooperate. It may not catch, cage, relocate, ban or punish. Everything in the second list belongs to the state, or to no one.

Owning a dog comes with a short stack of paperwork and a few rights people keep being talked out of.

Licence

Most municipal laws require the dog to be registered with the local body and its anti-rabies vaccination kept current. City-specific - check yours.

AWBI, 2015

A housing society cannot ban pets, bar them from lifts, or evict them by bye-law. The AWBI's 2015 advisory says so in terms, and courts and consumer forums have consistently relied on it.

2017 + 2018 Rules

Breeders must be registered under the Dog Breeding and Marketing Rules 2017, shops under the Pet Shop Rules 2018. An unregistered breeder is operating outside the law - do not buy from one.

Breed bans

The 2024 central circular against 23 so-called ferocious breeds was set aside by the Karnataka and Delhi High Courts within weeks, and the Centre told the Bombay High Court it would not be enforced. There is no national breed ban in force - though individual cities do impose local conditions, so check your municipality.

PCA s.11

Abandoning a dog is an offence. So is cosmetic mutilation - ear cropping and tail docking have no medical defence.

1978 Rules

Moving a dog across the country is governed by the Transport of Animals Rules - space, food, water and handling standards - and the railways carry dogs under their own conditions.

The law answers a bite with process, not panic - and it holds every side to account.

The owner

A negligent owner answers under BNS s.291 and in civil damages. The May 2026 judgment made accountability the price of asserting rights over any dog - owned, fed or maintained.

The victim

High Courts have pushed states toward compensation schemes for bite victims, anchored in the state's Article 21 duty to public safety. Immediate medical care and anti-rabies treatment come first, always.

The dog

A biting dog is assessed, not condemned. Rabid, incurably ill or demonstrably dangerous dogs follow the veterinary protocol above. The rest are sterilised, vaccinated and managed under the Rules.

The street

Retaliation is where the law is bluntest: beating or poisoning dogs after an incident is exactly what BNS s.325 punishes. Report the dog, treat the wound, let the process run.

Plain-language orientation, read from the statutes and the live court record - not legal advice, and the record moves. For a live case, an FIR being refused, or a feeder under threat, reach the PFA network and we will stand with you.

For veterinarians

You have the knowledge. This is where it reaches the people holding the animal.

A vet sees a hundred owners a week and can only tell each one once. Publish here, and you tell the next hundred thousand, in a form they will actually use.

What we publish, and how it is checked.

We are not a blog. Every piece is reviewed before it goes live and carries the author's name, qualification and registration. The bar is simple: would you stake your professional reputation on it.

  • You submit a draft, or an outline and we help shape it
  • A second veterinarian reviews it for accuracy
  • Every clinical claim is matched to a citation, or it comes out
  • It is published under your name, with your credentials and a link to your practice
  • You can update it whenever guidance changes. It stays yours
